Domine Eduard Osok Airport (also known as Sorong Airport) (IATA: SOQ, ICAO: WASS) is an airport in Sorong, West Papua, Indonesia. It is one of the largest and busiest airports on the Bird's Head Peninsula. It replaced the smaller, former World War II airfield, Jefman Airport on the island of Jefman. Domine Eduard Osok Airport serves as a major gateway to the Raja Ampat Islands, since it is closer to the city of Sorong, compared to the provincial capital of Manokwari. Due to increasing demand, the government has lengthen the runway to 2,500 m from 1,950 m so that the airport can accommodate Boeing 737 and Airbus A320. Terminal Facilities The new terminal could accommodate 2,4 million passenger per year, three times larger than the current terminal, which could only accommodate 700,000 passenger per year. A wide variety of shops and stalls are available throughout the Airport, including a mini market, clothes boutique, newsagent, restaurants, cafe, ATMs and a shop selling local handicrafts. The airport provides safe and secure short term and long term parking for your vehicle during your trip. The Airport is operated and managed by PT Angkasa Pura I. Flight Destinations Domine Eduard Osok Airport is served by few local airlines such as Garuda Indonesia, Lion Air, Susi Air and Wings Air with Ambon and Makassar are the most popular routes. The Airport is capable of accommodating aircraft such as the Boeing 737s and Airbus A320. Technical Specification The airport resides at an elevation of 3 m (10 ft) above mean sea level. It has one runway designated 09/27 with an asphalt surface measuring 2,500 m (6,398 ft) in length.
