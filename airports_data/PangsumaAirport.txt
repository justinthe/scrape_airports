Pangsuma Airport (IATA: PSU, ICAO: WIOP) is an airport in Putussibau, West Kalimantan, Indonesia. The Airport is named after the famous Dayak war leaders Pangsuma in West Kalimantan. The distance between the Airport and Putussibau is 3.7 km via Jl. Lintas Kalimantan Poros Utara. Putussibau is the capital of Kapuas Hulu within approximately 800 km from Pontianak. The town also becomes the gateway to the two major eco-tourism destination in the heart of Borneo, namely Sentarum National Park and Betung Kerihun National Park. The Airport is managed and controlled by by UPT Ditjen Hubud, an agency under Ministry of Transportation, Indonesia. Technical Specification Pangsuma Airport resides at an elevation of 91 m (297 ft) above mean sea level. It has one runway designated 10/28 with an asphalt surface measuring 1,004 m (3,294 ft).
