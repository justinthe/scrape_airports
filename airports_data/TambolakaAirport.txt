Tambolaka Airport (Indonesian: Bandar Udara Tambolaka) (IATA: TMC, ICAO: WADT) is an airport in Tambolaka, West Sumba Regency, East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia. The Airport is inaugurated a new terminal on 2015 which is able to improve passenger services from the previous old terminal. Cafe and shops are available at the terminal. There are some car parking lots and taxi stand outside the terminal. The Airport is managed and controlled by UPT Ditjen Hubud, an agency under Ministry of Transportation, Indonesia. Flight Destinations Tambolaka Airport is served by few local airlines daily such as Garuda Indonesia, TransNusa and Wings Air with Denpasar and Kupang are the most popular routes. The Airport is capable of accommodating aircraft such as the Airbus A320, Boeing 737-800 and Boeing 737-900. Airline Name Destination Garuda Indonesia Denpasar, Kupang TransNusa Denpasar, Ende, Kupang Wings Air Denpasar, Kupang Public Transportation Bus: No airport bus are serving from Tambolaka Airport. Private cars and taxi: Various private cars and taxi services are provided by numerous individual and service providers outside the arrival hall. Bargain for the price is possible. Technical Specification The airport is at an elevation of 203 ft (62 m) above mean sea level. It has one runway designated 10/28 with an asphalt surface measuring 2,300 m (7,546 ft).
