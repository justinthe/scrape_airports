Silangit International Airport (Indonesian: Bandar Udara Internasional Silangit) (IATA: DTB, ICAO: WIMN) is an airport located in Siborong-Borong, North Tapanuli, North Sumatra, Indonesia. Distance from the Prapat town is about 47 mi (76 km). The airport serves as the main gateway to the nearby Lake Toba. A highway linked between the Airport to Lake Toba has been planned and the construction work has been carried out.The operation of the Airport is managed by PT Angkasa Pura. Terminal Facilities With the inauguration of the well equipped and modern terminal on 15 September 2017, the Airport now able to provided better and comfortable environment for its passengers. The new terminal is able to increase the passenger capacity per year to more than half a million passengers. The new terminal has a modern design and is equipped with additional amenities such as shops, food courts, executive break room, internet facilities, disabled toilets, prayers room, smoking room, as well as a room mother and child. Flight Destinations There are commercial schedule flights from Silangit Airport to destinations as below: Airline Name Destination Batik Air Jakarta-Soekarno Hatta, Singapore Garuda Indonesia Jakarta-Soekarno-Hatta, Medan, Singapore Sriwijaya Air Jakarta-Soekarno-Hatta Susi Air Gunung Sitoli, Medan Wings Air Medan Public Transportation and Car Park Taxis and minivan taxi are available outside the arrival hall. The new parking facilities are now capable of accommodating 100 cars and 50 motorcycles and it is within walking distance outside the terminal. Technical Specification The airport resides at an elevation of 1,420 metres (4,660 ft) above mean sea level. It has one runway designated 09/27 with an asphalt surface measuring 2,650 m × 45 m (8,694 ft × 145 ft), capable of handling Airbus A320 and Boeing 737-800.
