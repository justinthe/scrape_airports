Fatmawati Soekarno Airport (IATA: BKS, ICAO: WIGG), formerly known as Padang Kemiling Airport, is an airport in Bengkulu, a city in the Bengkulu province of Indonesia. It is located 14 km from the city center. The airport is named after Fatmawati Soekarno Airport on 14 November 2001, the First Lady of Indonesia, wife of Soekarno, the first President of Indonesia. With the 2,470 m length runway, the airport able to accommodate the Airbus A320 and Boeing 737 aircraft. The Airport is operated and managed by UPT Ditjen Hubud.
